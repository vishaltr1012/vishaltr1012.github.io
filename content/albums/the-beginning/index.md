Photography has always fascinated me. 
There is something special in recording the day to day activities and memories as time frames and being able to revisit and cherish about the reminiscent past.
For almost eight years, owning a DSLR was a dream. On 23rd May 2026, that dream finally became a reality when I bought my first Canon mirrorless camera.
At first the excitement was high, but so was the confusion. The camera came with lot of features, modes, profile settings and adjustments. I wanted to learn everything at once such as aperture, shutter speed, ISO, autofocus modes and more. I was in a hustle to try out all the features at the same time but at the end my mental patience to test out each of them drained before the camera's capabilities were.

But what astonished me was people reactions when i walked the streets with camera in hand.
Some people looked at me in suspicion, wondering why i am taking photos.
Some thought me as a kid who had too much free time and wandering around their businesses
Many greeted me with a smile and happily posed when I asked for a photograph.
And a few even mistook me for a reporter and politely refused permission, which, honestly, was quite funny.

But that's the beauty of being a beginner. Every outing teaches some new things, not just in photography, but about the people, culture, local tastes, confidences and observations.

These are a few of my first photographs from visits to Thiruthani and Mylapore. They're far from perfect, but they mark the beginning of a journey that I've wanted to start for a very long time. 
Every journey has a beginning and this is my beginning.
